A Niyatna workspace is the controlled environment where a company request becomes owned work.

It is not just a folder or dashboard. It is the context boundary for one company, one department, one workflow, or one approved request.

---

## What a workspace holds

A workspace can include:

* the active OpenIntent request;
* company context and SOPs;
* role and permission rules;
* assigned humans or agents;
* allowed tools and data sources;
* review gates;
* proof artifacts;
* memory updates after completion.

## Company-wide access

Niyatna workspaces are designed for authorized company participants, not only one owner.

Examples:

* a finance lead requests a standard-based review;
* an operations member schedules a recurring handoff;
* a partner receives scoped status visibility;
* an auditor reviews proof artifacts;
* an agent completes a bounded task and reports back.

## HQ surface

Niyatna HQ should make work legible:

* what was requested;
* who owns it;
* what context is attached;
* what is allowed;
* what needs review;
* what proof was returned;
* what memory changed.

## Workspace principle

A good workspace reduces ambiguity. It should make it obvious what the company asked for, what the system is allowed to do, and what evidence is required before the work is accepted.
