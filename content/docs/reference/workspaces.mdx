A **workspace** is the boundary around a piece of company work.

It defines what context can be used, which tools are available, who owns the work, what review is required, and what proof must be returned.

---

## Workspace scope

A workspace may define:

* **Company or department:** Which part of the organization the work belongs to.
* **Requester:** Who asked for the work and under which role.
* **Assignee:** The human, agent, or mixed path responsible for the next step.
* **Allowed context:** Files, SOPs, records, memory, or submitted materials available to the workflow.
* **Allowed tools:** Systems that may be used for the active intent.
* **Review gate:** Who approves staged or risky actions.
* **Proof destination:** Where the result and evidence are stored.

## Workspace security contract

A Niyatna workspace should:

1. Keep work inside the approved scope.
2. Separate draft, staged, and accepted outputs.
3. Make risky actions wait for review.
4. Return proof before memory or operating records are updated.

## Workspace rule

The workspace should make the boundary visible. If a request does not define scope, owner, allowed tools, and proof, it is not ready for execution.
