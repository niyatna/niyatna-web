The **Delegation Trail** is the history of how an intent moved through the company system.

It records the path from request to assignment, execution, review, proof, memory update, and report-back. The goal is not to expose noisy raw logs; the goal is to make delegated work inspectable.

## Audit checklist

Before accepting completed work, Niyatna checks:

* **Intent alignment:** Did the result answer the original request?
* **Scope control:** Did the work stay inside its permission boundary?
* **Ownership:** Was the right human or agent assigned?
* **Review gate:** Was a risky step approved before completion?
* **Evidence:** Are the required artifacts attached?
* **Memory:** Should the outcome update company context?

## Reclaiming leverage

Delegation should reduce review burden, not create hidden work. A clear trail lets humans move faster because they can inspect the important decisions and evidence without replaying every intermediate action.
