Niyatna Route decides how an intent should move through the company system.

The route is not just a model picker. It is the decision layer that determines whether work should go to a human reviewer, an agent, a lightweight model, a stronger reasoning model, a company tool, or a gated review path.

---

## Routing inputs

Niyatna Route considers:

* the requested outcome;
* role and permission scope;
* sensitivity of the attached context;
* tool requirements;
* cost and latency needs;
* required proof standard;
* whether the action needs review before completion.

## Routing outcomes

A route may choose:

* a human-only path;
* an agent-assisted path;
* a low-risk automation;
* a higher-reasoning model path;
* a read-only tool path;
* a staged action that waits for approval.

## Route stability

The route can change over time, but the intent should stay stable. Niyatna keeps the company request, acceptance criteria, and proof requirement as the anchor while routing work through the best available execution path.

## Why it matters

Agentic companies need leverage without losing control. Niyatna Route is designed to choose the right execution path while preserving company boundaries, proof, and report-back.
