Niyatna is the **agentic-company formation system**: the operating layer, standards, gates, and interfaces for companies ready to run with agents.

A company does not become agentic just because it uses AI tools. It becomes agentic when company intent, people, agents, memory, permissions, recurring work, and proof operate as one governed system.

Niyatna turns company intent into owned work: routed to the right human or agent, grounded in company memory, checked through permission boundaries, and returned with evidence the company can audit.

> Turn company intent into verified work.

---

## Core architecture

The Niyatna system is organized around six public surfaces:

* **OpenIntent:** Converts a company request into a structured intent with goal, context, permissions, acceptance criteria, and proof requirements.
* **Niyatna OS:** Makes the company askable through role-aware channels and company surfaces.
* **Niyatna HQ:** Turns requests into owned work with assignee, due date, review gate, and report-back.
* **Niyatna Agents:** Executes scoped company work as accountable workforce units, not generic assistants.
* **Niyatna Memory:** Keeps SOPs, decisions, departments, customers, and proof history from resetting.
* **Proof of Intent:** Connects request, execution, evidence, review, memory update, and final report.

## The company loop

| Stage | Niyatna surface | What changes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Ask | Niyatna OS | Authorized people can ask the company system directly. |
| Assign | Niyatna HQ | Requests become owned work with review gates. |
| Execute | Niyatna Agents + Route | Work is routed to the right human, agent, model, or tool. |
| Remember | Niyatna Memory | Decisions, SOPs, and proof history remain available for the next request. |
| Prove | Proof of Intent | Work returns with evidence and report-back. |

## Why it matters

When AI makes problem-solving cheaper, more companies will try to delegate work to agents. The scarce thing becomes the system that keeps that delegation aligned with company standards, role boundaries, memory, and proof.

Niyatna is that formation system.
